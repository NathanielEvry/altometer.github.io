About Me Link to heading

Greetings! I’m Nathaniel Evry, a passionate technologist with a diverse set of skills and a love for innovation. With years of experience in the tech industry, I’ve built and implemented software systems for a variety of clients and projects. Currently, I serve as the Chief Research Officer at quub, a small aerospace startup. In this role, I bring my knowledge of miniaturized electronics, signal transmission and reception, hardware design, coding, architecture, and research to the table.

In my free time, I love to tinker with hardware, experiment with new programming languages, and explore the great outdoors. Philosophy, science fiction, and art are some of my other interests that I try to incorporate into my work whenever possible.

I have a vast range of skills including 3D printing, systems architecture, software engineering, data science, PCB and electronic engineering, hardware prototyping, and much more. I’ve worked with numerous technologies and enjoy staying up to date with the latest news, exploits, and toys.

Some fun facts about me: I was an early adopter of 3D printing and have printed, donated, and trained others on the assembly of 3D printed prosthetic hands for kids. I’ve built and raced high-speed FPV drones, and I’m a regular speaker and contributor to the Lancaster Open Source Community.

Thank you for visiting my blog. I’m excited to share my ideas, experiences, and musings with you, and I look forward to engaging in meaningful conversations with fellow technology and innovation enthusiasts. Don’t hesitate to reach out, and let’s connect!
