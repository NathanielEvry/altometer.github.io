New Blog, Who Dis?

April 10, 2023 3-minute read

New Blog, Who Dis? Link to heading

It’s a question that we’ve all asked at some point in our lives - whether we’re starting a new job, moving to a new city, or embarking on a new adventure. And it’s a question that I’m asking myself today, as I sit down to write my first post for this brand new blog.

My name is Nathaniel Evry, and I’m a passionate technologist with a diverse set of skills and a love for innovation. I’ve worked on a lot of amazing and near-magical technology projects over the years, but one thing that’s always held me back is the difficulty in maintaining all of the moving pieces of a website. That’s why I’m so excited to be launching this blog today - it’s going to be my own little corner of the internet where I can share and index my projects, promote my personal identity and web presence, and share my thoughts.

Sharing My Projects and Thoughts Link to heading

So what can you expect? Well, for starters, a highly sporadic posting schedule. I’ll be sharing some of the projects that I’ve worked on over the years, as well as some of the new projects I’m actively undertaking. I have a lot of irons in the fire, so I can’t promise to share everything, but I’m excited to showcase some of the cool things I’ve built.

But this blog won’t just be about technology and coding. I’ll also be sharing my thoughts on a wide variety of topics, from current events to philosophy to the nature of creativity itself. I believe that the best way to grow and learn is to engage with ideas and perspectives that are different from our own, so I’m excited to hear from readers who have their own thoughts and opinions to share.

Join Me on This Journey Link to heading

Ultimately, this blog is about creating a space for myself and others to explore and discover the vast and murky depths of the unknowns and all of technology, maths, ai, coding, random gadget reviews, and much more. It’s about embracing the freedom and flexibility that comes with having your own little corner of the internet, and using it to showcase our individuality and unique perspectives.

So thank you for taking the time to read my first post, and for joining me on this journey. I can’t wait to see where it takes us.

If you have any comments or questions, feel free to get in touch with me on twitter or LinkedIn.

Stay curious, stay open-minded, and stay true to yourself.

Cheers,

Nathaniel
